## Directory/category footer full-bleed — DONE (2026-06-01, `7a1baf0`)
The white desktop footer card on the directory/category routes (`/brand`, `/shops`, `/discover`, `/category/*`) was NOT full-bleed like home — measured live at viewport 1940, the footer's `whiteCard` was capped + centered at 1440 (L250 R1690 W1440), leaving gray gutters on both sides.
- **Root cause:** the footer DOES break out to full-bleed via negative margin + a full-width white bg, but an ancestor `ScrollView` inside the screen's 1440-capped `phoneFrame` has `overflowX:hidden` at width 1440, which CLIPPED the footer's full-bleed white back to the centered 1440 window. (Home was already correct because `ce8de6a` mounts its footer in a full-width shell OUTSIDE the cap.)
- **Fix:** applied the same proven home pattern — on desktop, lift the footer out of the 1440 cap. The screen mounts a full-width shell + full-bleed `ScrollView`; page content is wrapped in a 1440-centered `desktopContentCap`; the footer sits in an uncapped `desktopFooterCap` rendering `<CustomerDesktopFooter horizontalPadding={0} viewportWidth={width}/>` directly (not the Slot). The mobile branch is unchanged (forked on `isDesktop`). Touched 2 screens: `CustomerCategoryDetailScreen.tsx` and `CustomerDiscoveryScreen.tsx` (the latter backs the `/brand`, `/shops`, `/discover` sub-screens).
- **Verified after** (live `:8081`, vw=1940): footer `whiteCard` now L0 R1940 W1940 (full-bleed) on `/brand`, `/shops`, `/discover`, and `/category/Travel` — matching home; page content still caps + centers at 1440 (`contentCap` L250 R1690 W1440); no horizontal overflow (`scrollWidth == innerWidth`); mobile `/brand` @390 unchanged.
